# Progression & Research

This document summarizes the progression system from the HLDD: **time-based skill training** (no XP grinding) and **resource-based research** in four disciplines. Loot and effort from the Macro Loop (docs/01_core_loops.md) feed into both. Research is conducted in the Home System (docs/02_home_system.md); the four branches are also summarized there.

---

## 1. Time-Based Skill Training (Retention Hook)

Progression is **not** based on XP or grinding actions. It is based on real-time **Skill Training Queues** (HLDD §7.1).

### Mechanics

- **Passive training:** Players select skills, add them to a training queue. Skills train **passively in real time**, even when the player is offline (HLDD §7.1).
- **No XP from actions:** Killing or mining does not directly level the character; it provides resources to use the skills you have trained (HLDD §7.1).
- **24-hour queue limit:** The queue has a **strict 24-hour limit**. Players must **log in daily** to replenish it with new skills. If the queue runs dry, training stops and time is wasted (HLDD §7.1).

### Strategic Choice (Examples from HLDD §7.1)

Time is the most valuable resource. Players must prioritize what to train (HLDD §7.1):

| Skill | Effect (HLDD §7.1) |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Mining Proficiency** | Decreases cycle time of mining lasers. |
| **Wormhole Physics** | Reduces the amount of Entropy a ship generates. |
| **Fleet Command** | Increases the maximum number of ships controllable in a sortie. |

### Other Skills Referenced in the HLDD

- **Wormhole Navigation:** Reduces **exfiltration spool-up time** (HLDD §4.6; docs/03_ogate_mechanics.md).
- **Sensor Analysis:** Extends the **Detection Horizon** (earlier Proximity Alerts against incoming warps) (HLDD §4.4; docs/04_combat_and_detection.md).
- **Engineering:** High levels reduce the **severity of module damage and Specialist injury time** when escaping via Emergency Transponder (HLDD §5.3; docs/06_risk_and_loss.md).

## 2. Research Branches (The Tech Tree)

Research improves the **empire** (infrastructure, hulls, modules, OGate). It is divided into **four distinct disciplines**, each with its own progression tree (HLDD §7.2). Research is conducted in the Home System and costs resources (Ore/Biomass/Energy) and time.

### Home System Research (Economy & Infrastructure)

- **Focus:** Maximizing the efficiency and output of the Safe Zone (HLDD §7.2).
- **Examples:** Solar Efficiency, Bio-Combustion Output, Refinery Yields, Diplomatic Protocols (improves Faction Standing gains), Automated Defense Grids (HLDD §7.2).

### Blueprint Research (The Armory)

- **Focus:** Unlocking new Hulls and Contingent types (HLDD §7.2).
- **Examples:** Cruiser Hulls, Strategic Lancer Chassis, Marine Detachment Training, Fighter Squadron Protocols (HLDD §7.2).

### Tech Center (Modules & Materials)

- **Focus:** Upgrading ship equipment and processing exotic materials (HLDD §7.2).
- **Examples:** Fusion Containment (Reactors/Lances), Deep Core Mining Lasers, Polymer Synthesis (Advanced Alloys), Shield Harmonics, Compression Technology (HLDD §7.2).

### OGate Inc. (Wormhole Science)

- **Focus:** Mastery of the OGate mechanism itself (HLDD §7.2).
- **Examples:** Capacitor Science (unlocks 1GW+ tiers), Entropy Shielding (reduces Rift instability), Jump Calculation Speed (faster entry/exit), Anomaly Scanning (better radar resolution) (HLDD §7.2).

## 3. Advanced Progression (High-End Economy)

The HLDD defines two high-end progression currencies that are not bought with real money (HLDD §7.3).

### Nanites (The Forbidden Tech)

- **Lore:** Forbidden technology from the Previous Era (HLDD §7.3).
- **Mechanic:** Required to attach powerful **Cybernetic Enhancements** to the player character (global bonuses to skills) (HLDD §7.3).
- **Economy:** Nanites **cannot be bought** with real money or premium currency. They must be **extracted from high-risk OGate instances**. This forces high-spenders to play or trade with skilled players (HLDD §7.3).

### Precursor Artifacts (The Prestige Economy)

- **Mechanic:** Rare, non-functional relics found in Deep Space (HLDD §7.3).
- **The Trader:** A unique NPC in the Home System accepts Artifacts in exchange for **Exclusive Skins** and **Visual Customizations** (HLDD §7.3).
- **Exclusivity:** These cosmetics are **unavailable in the cash shop**. A “Void-Touched” hull skin proves the player survived deep space, not that they paid (HLDD §7.3).
